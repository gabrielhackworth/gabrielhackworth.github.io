AGS

Before I came to Austin High, I was in the magnet program at Kealing Middle School. Having such a small community surrounding me was an experience I wanted to have again, and that was what drew me to the Academy for Global Studies.

My mom's side moved frequently, from Guam to Virginia to Belgium. She's half Filipino and half Puerto Rican, and as a result grew up steeped in multiculturalism. My dad's side is 100% Scottish immigrants, but still traveled all over North America, from Florida to Nova Scotia. We travel to many places both inside and outside the US for vacations, and I also really looked forward to traveling for school when I applied for AGS. The experiences I have had at the Heifer International Ranch and Costa Rica have been very interesting and informative, and I believe that AGS has exposed me to a great deal of things that I would not have otherwise seen.

Service

When I was a freshman, the 80 hour service requirement seemed an impossible goal. Since then, I've found organizations to volunteer with that I have greatly enjoyed doing so with. Three of my summers in high school I spent volunteering at an Austin Public Library branch for 4+ hours a week. They tend to be understaffed, and since I spent so much time at them when I was younger I decided to give back to them.

Volunteering with TreeFolks introduced me to just how important sustainability is, and I've spent several weekends planting saplings and tree cuttings along the greenbelt or in city parks.

The Goodwill GreenWorks program allowed me to take something I'm already good at and use it to give back to the community. I would help repair computers donated to Goodwill, and prepare them for resale at low prices. Unfortunately, the program has since been discontinued.

Through community service, I have gained an appreciation for all that I have. I plan to continue volunteering throughout the rest of my life and give back to the society that has served me for the past 18 years.
